ADOPTIONS

One of our four big needs is adoption. What makes that one of the big four? Adoptions allow us to help abandoned and unwanted cats and dogs find a loving home. With each adoption, a life is saved.

Also with each adoption the shelter, through the adoption fee, gets the much needed financial support to continue its lifesaving efforts.

Funding Support

Pet Welfare is a 501(c)(3) tax exempt, charitable, nonprofit organization. Cash gifts are very important to Pet Welfare and are the easiest way to support our work. You can simply write a check or send a money order. Donations are deductible to the full extent allowed by law.

100% of all monetary donations go directly to maintain the shelter and to provide a safe and healthy environment for the animals while they wait to be adopted into a forever home of their own!

Pet Welfare will put your gift to work immediately on behalf of our animals.

The donations are used for:

Veterinary services (rabies shots, spay/neutering, medical procedures) Cat and dog food (includes special diet foods as prescribed by a vet) Supplies (cleaning material, cat litter, office supplies)

WHY

Why Get a Shelter Pet?

Why Get a Shelter Pet?

Why get a pet from an animal shelter?

They are your best source when looking for a pet. Pet Welfare has a great selection of cats and dogs that are already spayed or neutered and up to date on all shots and you can see them all at our Pet Finder pages.

At Pet Welfare we screen all animals that come to our shelter. We ensure every animal is evaluated for temperament. The volunteers give special attention to every "new" resident to the shelter to make them feel at home during their adjustment period. During the interview process the adopter will ask many questions to ensure that the pet you are thinking about adopting is the best match for you, your family, and situation. We at Pet Welfare take each and every adoption very seriously.

Come in and meet our community of cats and dogs - we are sure if you are ready to adopt that we have the right pet for you and your family.

Other Ways To Donate

Other Ways To Donate

We always welcome and truly appreciate your cash gifts. Did you know that there are other ways to give and support Pet Welfare? Please consider gift-giving plans as an integral part of your total estate planning. We encourage you to discuss these methods with your financial and legal advisors to determine a strategy that is most appropriate for you. Bequest - By naming Pet Welfare in your will, you leave a legacy without giving up assets during your lifetime. A specific bequest in your will may help you save on estate taxes.

Final Act of Love

Final Act of Love

To our friends who wish to remember Pet Welfare in their Wills, the following form is recommended: "I hereby give, devise and bequeath to Pet Welfare, whose shelter is located on Range Road, Eglin Air Force Base, Florida, 32542, the sum of $_________. These funds are to be used for the purpose of operating and maintaining the shelter as determined by the Pet Welfare Board of Directors." (It is suggested that you take this form to your attorney so that your specific request may be incorporated into your will.) Living Trust - You (or a beneficiary) receive the income from trust assets, and you name who will ultimately receive the remainder of the trust assets. Since the trust assets bypass probate, the terms are private. Insurance Policy - You may be able to take a charitable donation when you name Pet Welfare as beneficiary and assign us ownership of a life insurance policy. And since the proceeds are removed from your estate, you may benefit from reduced estate taxes. Stocks and Bonds - There are no capital gains taxes on the appreciation in value, no second tax on accumulated earnings by averting a dividend distribution, and you may benefit from an income tax deduction for the charitable contribution.
